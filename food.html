Food

FDA Warning: Please read the FDA report on dietary DCM!

(The beef heart above is a great source of Taurine!)

I learned to read labels for fresh human grade ingredients in my dog's food and support 'made in the USA' companies.

Vets are usually not nutritionists, so they don't have the market cornered on best food choices for your dog.

I am hoping better 'Human Grade' dog food companies start to produce prescription diets, so our pets can have a higher quality and healthier food choice while supporting their special needs.

RAW – FRESH – HOMEMADE

Beef Heart

GREAT for much needed TAURINE in a dog's diet! I get chunked from the local butcher and flash boil a few seconds to kill any surface bacteria — the center is extremely rare!

Eggs

Soft or hard boil.

Liver

Boil or bake. Make sure not to give too much liver — it is high in Vitamin A and can cause loose stools if you give too much.

Sardines

In water from the grocery store — a nice addition.

RMB (Raw Meaty Bones)

I get frozen femurs from the butcher and the marrow is the best for dogs!

RAW Mixtures (inc. TRIPE)

From the butcher — can also be purchased containing all the nutrients a dog needs. You can feed it raw or slightly cooked!

Tip: Save the meat juice for ice cubes or a warm topper for your pup!

Brand Recommendations

Carna4

100% real food recipes contain no synthetics of any kind and no premixes! Also they offer nutritional power of organic sprouted seeds!

Nature's Logic

100% natural and made from trusted meat sources. It does not use peas, lentils, or legumes that prevent Taurine absorption.

The Honest Kitchen

(Raw Dehydrated) This grain inclusive dog food combines organic oats and barley with ranch raised beef for a low fat, moderate protein meal perfect for adult and senior dogs. Especially suited for weight management.

Open Farm

Knowing your pet is getting the best nutrition possible — animal welfare certified meats raised without antibiotics or added hormones, sustainable seafood, non-GMO fruits and veggies, and more.

Warning: Human grade food is what our dogs need too! Read ingredients stringently, especially in Peanut butter and soft dog treats! Stay away from Xylitol and Propylene glycol. Research ingredients that are foreign to you!!!
